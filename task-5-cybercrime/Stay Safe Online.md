General Safety Practices



1. Think before you click
2. Avoid opening links or attachments from unknown emails, messages, or social media accounts.
3. Verify the sender and website
4. Always double-check email IDs, URLs, and profiles before trusting or responding.
5. Use strong, unique passwords
6. Create different passwords for each account using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
7. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
8. Add an extra layer of security to email, social media, and banking apps.
9. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
10. Do not access banking or personal accounts on unsecured networks like cafés or open campus Wi-Fi.
11. Limit personal information sharing
12. Avoid posting phone numbers, addresses, ID proofs, or travel plans online.



Financial / UPI Safety (Important)



1. Never share OTP, UPI PIN, or banking details
2. No legitimate service will ask for these — sharing them can lead to immediate fraud.
3. Check UPI requests carefully before approving
4. A “collect request” means money will be debited from your account, not received.
5. Use only official apps and payment links
6. Download apps from trusted app stores and avoid unknown payment links.



Reporting \& Immediate Action



1. Report cyber fraud immediately
2. File a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
3. Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
4. Use this number for quick assistance, especially in financial fraud cases.
5. Act quickly to minimize damage
6. Block your bank account, change passwords, and inform your bank immediately after detecting suspicious activity.
